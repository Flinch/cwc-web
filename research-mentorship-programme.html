How the CWC Research Mentorship Programme is Transforming Nigeria's Next Generation of Public Health Leaders

December 11, 2023

From Professionals to Change Makers

Leadership is rarely born in isolation. It is cultivated through guidance, challenge, encouragement, and opportunities to grow. At CWC Research, mentorship has proven to be a powerful catalyst for transforming National Health Fellows (NHF) into confident, resilient, and innovative leaders capable of driving meaningful change across Nigeria's health system.

The mentorship component of the National Health Fellows Programme was designed to provide more than technical support. It created a safe space where fellows could reflect, learn, adapt, and develop the confidence to lead in complex environments. Through continuous engagement with experienced mentors, fellows discovered strengths they never knew they possessed and developed the capacity to turn challenges into opportunities.

Redefining Identity and Unlocking Potential

Many fellows began their journey viewing themselves as health professionals, technical experts, or observers within the system. Through mentorship, they started seeing themselves differently.

As one fellow reflected:

"I discovered potentials in me that I never knew existed—confidence, empathy, leadership, and advocacy."

Mentorship empowered fellows to move beyond their professional titles and embrace their identities as community leaders and change agents. Fellows who were once timid and hesitant became confident advocates, standing before traditional leaders, local government officials, and state commissioners to champion improvements in healthcare delivery.

Building Problem-Solvers and Adaptive Leaders

The programme's structured mentorship model provided continuous guidance and psychosocial support through regular check-ins, reflective exercises, peer learning sessions, and the STAR methodology (Situation, Task, Action, Result).

One fellow described the experience as transformative:

"Mentorship became a turning point for me. Having someone to guide my thinking, challenge my approach, and support me through uncertainty made a significant difference."

The result was a new generation of adaptive leaders capable of navigating uncertainty, solving real-world problems, and mobilizing communities around shared goals.

Mentors witnessed this transformation firsthand. One mentor observed:

"At first, he was reserved and cautious, waiting for formal approval. Now, he proactively solves problems, mobilizes stakeholders, and drives projects forward."

These experiences demonstrate that mentorship does more than transfer knowledge—it builds confidence, resilience, and initiative.

Developing Confidence and Collaboration Skills

Mentorship also helped fellows develop critical interpersonal skills.

One fellow who described herself as "painfully shy" learned, with her mentor's support, how to engage market leaders and diaspora groups as partners in development. She eventually secured land for a health post inside a local market—an achievement that once seemed unimaginable.

This journey exemplifies one of mentorship's most significant outcomes: enabling fellows to negotiate effectively, build partnerships, and recognize opportunities where others see barriers.

Turning Passion into Purpose

For many fellows, CWC Research Mentorship Programme provided the bridge between aspiration and action.

Safiya Adamu from Niger State recalled:

"When I was selected, I was frightened but honoured. I had the ideas, but I needed guidance on how to bring them to life. The mentorship I received was the turning point; it gave me the direction I needed when things felt unclear."

By helping fellows convert ideas into structured interventions, CWC Research Mentorship Programme fostered practical leadership and empowered them to create sustainable, community-led solutions.

Inspiring Bigger Visions for Impact

Perhaps the most profound impact of the CWC Research Mentorship Programme was its ability to expand how fellows imagined their own potential.

Babangida Muhammed entered the programme as a technology professional who considered himself "just an IT guy." Through mentorship, he realized that his digital expertise could become a tool for social justice and public health transformation.

He went on to develop WeRise, an innovative digital platform that allows rural communities to report health facility challenges in real time.

Reflecting on his journey, he said:

"I no longer see myself as merely a technology specialist. I am a public health leader who bridges digital innovation with lived realities."

His vision now extends beyond his immediate assignment, with aspirations to collaborate with international organizations and scale digital solutions across the state.

A Model for Sustainable Leadership

The stories emerging from the National Health Fellows Programme demonstrate that mentorship is not simply an add-on to leadership development—it is the engine that drives transformation.

Through the CWC Research Mentorship Programme, fellows became more than programme participants. They became confident leaders, problem-solvers, negotiators, innovators, and advocates for stronger health systems and healthier communities.

Their journeys are proof that when young professionals are trusted, supported, challenged, and guided, they develop the capacity to lead meaningful change.

CWC Research Mentorship Programme did more than provide advice. It transformed fellows into confident, resilient leaders capable of driving community and health system transformation across Nigeria.
